# ATUONA — Film Festival Strategy 2026
*A curated map of where this work belongs and why*

---

## Why This Matters

ATUONA is not AI-generated content. It is an authored cinematic system:
- Real underground Russian poetry by Elena Revicheva
- 11-module knowledge architecture (Gauguin, Impressionists, Atlas Shrugged, fashion, recovery, Polynesia, NFT, auction, vibe coding)
- Recurring authored characters — Kira Velerevich and Ule Glensdagen
- Fashion/editorial treatment layer (Luma Modify Video Director's Cut)
- Autonomous but curated pipeline

The positioning principle: this is not "AI made a video." This is a poet who built a film director agent and is using it to make work that could not exist any other way.

**One selection at a credible festival is worth more for professional positioning than 100,000 Instagram followers.**

### 3. Zebra Poetry Film Festival
**Berlin, Germany | June 4–7, 2026**

**Why this is the most important genre-specific festival:**
The Zebra is the world's largest festival dedicated exclusively to poetry film. Hosted by Haus für Poesie in Berlin — one of the most respected poetry institutions in Europe. Running for 20+ years. Receives approximately 2,000 submissions annually. If your work is selected here, you are officially recognised as a serious poetry filmmaker at the highest level of the genre.

This is the festival that was built for exactly what ATUONA produces. Underground Russian poetry turned into short film. This is the format Zebra was designed to celebrate.

- Max length: 12 minutes
- Films produced after January 1, 2024

**Action for ATUONA:**
The 2026 deadline has passed. Begin preparing for **Zebra 2027** immediately — it will be the flagship submission. Start a proper 5–8 minute poetry film now so it is ready by November 2026 for the December 2026 open call.

## What Every Submission Needs

### 1. A finished film (not a clip reel)
- Title card
- Poem text on screen (30–60 sec)
- Visual film (2–5 min for most festivals)
- End card: Director name, AI tools, year, project name
- **Burnt-in Russian/English subtitles** — required for all festivals

### 2. Vimeo Pro account
Standard for festival screener links. Password-protected. Clean presentation. ~$20/month or $84/year.

### 3. FilmFreeway account
[filmfreeway.com](https://filmfreeway.com) — used by virtually all festivals listed here. Free to create.

### 4. Film metadata (needed for every submission)
- Title
- Director: Elena Revicheva
- Country: Panama / Russia
- Year: 2026
- Runtime
- Genre: Experimental / Artists' Film / Poetry Film
- Synopsis (200–300 words, specific to this film, not a project pitch)
- Film poster / production still

### 5. Director's Statement (most important piece)

The director's statement is what separates a serious artist using AI from an AI content generator. Every festival programmer reads hundreds of AI film submissions that say "I used Stable Diffusion to..."

Yours must say something like:

> *ATUONA began as underground poetry written in Panama during a period of personal exile. The AI system that made this film was not a tool I used — it was a co-author I built. It holds 11 embedded knowledge modules on Gauguin's final years in the Marquesas, Impressionist color theory, Atlas Shrugged, art auction history, recovery, and fashion. When I give it a poem, it does not generate a video. It reads the poem against all 11 modules and decides what the frame should mean. The fashion/editorial layer is not aesthetic decoration — it is the character Kira, who writes for fashion magazines under a pseudonym, living inside the visual. I do not post-edit the films. I wrote the poem. I built the system. The system made the frame. That is the collaboration.*

## Choosing the First Film to Submit

**Criteria for the first submission:**
1. The poem that already has the strongest visualization (best image, best base video, completed Director's Cut)
2. The poem that has the most universal emotional content — not the most Russia-specific
3. The poem that is cleanest as a standalone work — does not require knowing the full book to feel complete

**Strong candidates to evaluate:**
- Page #087 "Красная собака" — visually strong, Gauguin connection is internationally legible, the red dog as metaphor is striking
- Any poem centered on exile, memory, or loss — these translate across cultures and are what festival programmers respond to

**Duration target:** 3–5 minutes for Aesthetica / Encounters. Under 12 minutes for Zebra (2027).

**The real advantage:**
Nobody else is submitting a film that was made by a poet who built the AI system herself, with an authored narrative world, knowledge-routed subtext, and a fashion/editorial pass on every frame. That is a genuinely distinct position. The statement has to communicate that clearly — the work does not explain itself.
